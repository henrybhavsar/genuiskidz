Most of the people are unaware about the benefits associated with abacus course for kids. Definitely the course ensures an increase in speed of the arithmetic calculations with accurate results but, Master Mind Abacus does lot more for kids at an early age. It develops an ability to utilize the left and right brain functions simultaneously. Below mentioned are the skills that get augmented in the program.

Develops Logical Understanding

While a child visualize the questions on the imaginary abacus after listening to it, they have to apply logic to do the proper movement of beads in the mind. This ability also helps the kids to apply logics in various situations and even in real-life scenarios.

Builds a Photographic Memory

When the beads movement is done in the mind, children pictures the position of the beads displayed in their mind. Unless the picture is created they would not be able to answer the question that was asked. Photographic memory enables a student capture the images or instances as it is in their memory.

Increases Concentration & Listening Skills

For every question to be answered correctly, children have to listen to the sum that is being read and concentrate on it so as to visualize it in their mind. This becomes a habit when exercised regularly throughout the abacus training for a period of 2 and half years. It applies to all subjects by imbibing better listening skills.

Ability to Recall

At the end, the children recall the picture of the abacus with relevant bead movements, and answer confidently and accurately. Recalling is most important for students as they may study too much but unless they can recall, they cannot score good, either in abacus or in any other examination.

Enhances Visualization

The children are trained to visualize the abacus and move imaginary beads in their mind when the sums are read out to them. By continuous practice, they learn to visualize and imagine whatever has been taught, like diagrams in biology, maps in geography or any other subject. It also applies to activities other than studies.
